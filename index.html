The Steady March of Consumer Debt

The financial crisis of 2007-2008 is considered the worst since the Great Depression. Caused by subprime lending, many institutions have stopped extending loans to subprime borrowers, but this has not fixed the problem of overlending. Consumer debt continues to climb, and the financial recovery looks like the beginning of another crisis.
